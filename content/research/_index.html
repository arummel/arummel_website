How does temperature affect complex locomotor processes across levels of biological organization?

Temperature has an unavoidable effect on virtually all biological processes and is often a critical determinant of biological performance at multiple levels. Muscle links physiology and performance.

Bat flight as a model system

Bats are the only mammals capable of powered flight and find themselves in a unique thermal environment. Since they are primarily active at night their wings are vulnerable to substantial radiative and conductive heat loss, even in warm ambient conditions. Despite this, bat wing muscles are so poorly insulated as to be clearly visible through the skin, and the distal muscles cool substantially during flight. How do bats maintain flight performance if their flight muscles are consistently cool? Muscle function declines when muscles operate at cool temperatures, which could slow muscle contractile rates and interfere with the flight stroke.
